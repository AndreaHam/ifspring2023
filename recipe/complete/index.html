Korean Potato Cheese
Corn Dog

Total Time: 30 minutes
Prep Time:5 minutes
Cook Time:15 minutes

Korean corn dogs,
also known as gamja hot dogs are a beloved savory, sweet street food found across Korea.

The classics
are hot dogs coated with a chewy and thick batter made of rice flour.

INGREDIENTS (12 Servings)

POTATOES
Peeled and cut into 1/4 in 6mm

BATTER
2 Cups all purpose flour
3 Tablespoons granulated sugar
1 Tablespoon baking powder
½ Teaspoon kosher salt
1 Cup whole milk, plus 3 tablespoons
1 Large beaten egg

ASSEMBLY
6 All-beef hot dogs
2 Shredded low moisture mozzarella cheeses
2 Cups panko breadcrumbs
2 Cups korean rice puffs
¼ Cup granulated sugar (for sprinkling)

SAUCE
Ketchup, yellow mustard

SPECIAL EQUIPMENT
Wooden skewers

INSTRUCTIONS

1 Preparing potatoes
-Sprinkle the flour over the boiled potatoes and toss until well coated.

2 Making Batter
-In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, baking powder, and salt.
-Whisk together 1 cup of milk and the egg until well combined. Mix wet and dry well.

3 Coating with Batter
-Dip a cheese and hot dog skewer into the cup with the batter, coating completely.
-Gently shake off any excess batter.

4 Coating with Potato
-Roll a battered dog in the floured potatoes.
-Then, immediately roll the potato-coated dog in the panko bread crumbs until completely coated.

5 Frying
-Fry the coated dogs in hot oil for 2-3 minutes, until golden brown and crispy. Then, sprinkle all over with sugar.

6 Drizzling Sauce
-Drizzle ketchup and mustard (or any sauce of your choice) over the corn dogs.

How to keep leftovers
These Korean corn dog with potatoes can last 4-5 days in a resealable container or bag in the fridge. You can also freeze them and reheat them in a microwave (2-3 minutes) or oven (15-20 minutes at 350 degrees) for later.

Tips from others
1. Make sure you dice the potatoes very small, this helps them stick!
2. Becareful of the cheese, if it starts to leak into the oil take it out.
3. Be quick when you add the toppings. The dough slids quickly over.
